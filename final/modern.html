Early 19th Century

Opium use was still quite popular in the early 1900s, despite being outlawed in 1914, and opium dens were still found throughout the country. For example, in “In the Opium Den,” the author writes of a photographer named Isaiah Taber who explored the opium dens of Chinatown. (Lee 172) During this time the railroads were just about done being built and many Chinese-Americans were being let go and had turned to secret, underground opium dens for comfort. These places were generally frowned upon because the dens were typically unsanitary and full of inebriated ‘Chinamen’ who were considered lesser people by the prejudiced majority.

Final Note

I have already mentioned in a previous page the medicinal benefits of opiates. Truth be told, their only benefit is their downfall. They numb you. This is necessary for complex surgical procedures and chronic pain. Unfortunately, opiates have been a consistent point of pressure for many individuals who are predisposed to addictive behaviors. Many people think they can ‘just try it once’, but eventually find themselves falling into the rabbit hole. If they’re lucky they can quit it, but their brain chemistry is forever altered. At worst that first try becomes their last. So be mindful, be careful, and be safe.
