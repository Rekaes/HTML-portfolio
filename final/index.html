Opium (Lachryma Papaveris)

Opium is an archaic drug. It has an extensive history, not only in the United States but also throughout the world. Many people have succumbed to its spell and been made to live in the shell of a life, enthralled by the claws of addiction. Simultaneously the drug has paved the road to medical advancements since the early days of humanity by being used as a pain relief medication or an anesthetic.

Why Opium?

My decision to research the ‘Milk of Paradise’ was initially attributed to a study conducted by Lee Robins regarding opium use by American soldiers in the Vietnam War, however, I don’t intend to use his research in my essay. The purpose of these writings is to examine and form an opinion on the historical, legal, scientific, and societal/cultural influences opium has had throughout its existence as a drug used and often abused by mankind.

Disclaimer

Opiates are highly addictive and are a deteriorative substance no matter what form they are taken in, they should only be used when directed by a medical professional and sparingly so.
